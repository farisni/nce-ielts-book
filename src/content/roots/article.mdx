## Why Vocabulary Matters

Building a strong vocabulary is the **foundation** of language mastery. Research shows that knowing just 2,000 word families covers nearly 80% of everyday English.

> "Without grammar very little can be conveyed; without vocabulary nothing can be conveyed."
> — David Wilkins

## The Power of Word Roots

Understanding word roots unlocks thousands of words at once. Consider these examples:

- **-spect-** (look) → inspect, prospect, spectacle, spectator
- **-dict-** (say) → predict, dictate, verdict, contradict
- **-duct-** (lead) → conduct, deduce, introduce, produce

### A Deeper Example: -prim-

The root `-prim-` means *first* or *primary*. From this single root we get:

1. **primary** — first in order or importance
2. **prime** — of the best quality
3. **premier** — first in rank
4. **primitive** — from the earliest times

## Learning Strategy

The most effective approach combines:

| Method | Effectiveness | Time Required |
|--------|--------------|---------------|
| Root Analysis | ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Medium |
| Spaced Repetition | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Low |
| Reading in Context | ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | High |

### Recommended Workflow

1. Learn the root meaning
2. Study 3–5 derived words
3. Practice with example sentences
4. Review after 1 day, 3 days, 7 days
